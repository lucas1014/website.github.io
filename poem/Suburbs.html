My street (the one I grew up on)
Lakemont Blvd, Cougar Mountain,
Pacific Northwest

Back Home


Our suburb is quiet
There is nothing interesting about our suburb, when we're
seventeen, eighteen, nineteen
and trying to remember how our grandparents left the CID
I remember
my favorite bedtime memory--
falling asleep to the soft bang of fireworks
because the smile tickling my small face
leaves me dreaming of celebrations, and

our markets are our sidewalks
Lemonade stands in front of front lawns
Where Jackson St. would expect
temples, we have greenbelts
so dense and thick the foliage,
that you can lose yourself in thought

In the distance, blue-green,
the original skyscrapers, 5000-some ft. tall
their true names are as old as time

No freeway; no airport; the streets
are wide and deserted, and as
night falls, the air descends, winds whisper
silence
for the quiet existence
